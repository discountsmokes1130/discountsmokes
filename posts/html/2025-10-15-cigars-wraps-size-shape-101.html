Cigar Sizes Explained

Cigars come in various sizes, typically categorized by their length and ring gauge. The length is measured in inches, while the ring gauge indicates the diameter in 64ths of an inch. For example, a cigar with a 50 ring gauge is 50/64ths of an inch wide.

  • Shorter Cigars: Perfect for a quick smoke, shorter cigars like coronas or petit robustos are often enjoyed by those who want a flavorful experience without the time commitment of a longer smoke.

  • Longer Cigars: For those who relish a more extended smoking session, larger formats like churchills and double coronas provide a rich, gradual unfolding of flavors.

Cigar Shapes to Consider

The shape of a cigar can greatly impact your experience. Here are a few popular shapes you can find at Discount Smokes:

  • Figurado: These cigars have a tapered end and come in various forms, like torpedoes and pyramids. They can offer a complex smoking experience as the tapered end allows for a concentrated flavor.
