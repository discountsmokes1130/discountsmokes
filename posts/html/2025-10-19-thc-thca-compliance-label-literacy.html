What‚Äôs the Difference?

THC (tetrahydrocannabinol) and THCa (tetrahydrocannabinolic acid) are two terms that often appear on product labels. THC is the well-known compound responsible for the psychoactive effects commonly associated with cannabis. On the other hand, THCa is the non-psychoactive precursor to THC. When cannabis is heated or decarboxylated (like when you smoke or cook with it), THCa converts into THC.

Understanding this difference is crucial, especially if you're interested in the effects you're seeking. Products high in THCa may offer different experiences than those with a higher THC content.
