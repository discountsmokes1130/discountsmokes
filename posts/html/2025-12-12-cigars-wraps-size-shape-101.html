Cigars are typically measured in two ways: length and ring gauge. Length is the measurement in inches, while the ring gauge is the diameter of the cigar, measured in 64ths of an inch. For example, a cigar with a ring gauge of 50 is 50/64 of an inch in diameter. These measurements determine how much tobacco is packed into the cigar and can affect the draw, burn, and flavor profile.
